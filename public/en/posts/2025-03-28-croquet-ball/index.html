Croquet ball

TLDR

A croquet ball named Bartholomew accidentally gets trapped inside a giant pumpkin. His frantic attempts to escape create a bizarre “singing” phenomenon noticed by the pumpkin’s owner. The Little Puddleton Harvest Festival is about to get a very unexpected, bouncy surprise.

The croquet ball, Bartholomew, was having a crisis. Not an existential one, mind you. Bartholomew was a perfectly content croquet ball, enjoying his life of being whacked across meticulously manicured lawns. No, this was a spatial crisis. He’s somehow ended up inside Mrs. Higgins’ prize-winning pumpkin.

It had all started innocently enough. A particularly enthusiastic whack from young Timmy Higgins had sent Bartholomew soaring, a glorious arc of red against the late afternoon sun. He’s misjudged the trajectory, however, and landed with a muffled thunk inside the gargantuan pumpkin, destined for the annual Little Puddleton Harvest Festival.

Now, Bartholomew was wedged. He could see the world through a blurry orange haze, hear the muffled chatter of Mrs. Higgins fretting about the pumpkin’s structural integrity. “It’s just… different today, Harold,” she’s saying to her bewildered husband. “It has a… resonance.”

Harold, a man who considered beige a vibrant colour, just shrugged. “Probably just the soil, dear.”

Bartholomew, meanwhile, was desperately trying to bounce his way out, resulting in a series of increasingly frantic thumps from within the pumpkin. Mrs. Higgins gasped. “Harold! It’s singing!”

The Little Puddleton Harvest Festival was going to be interesting, to say the least. Bartholomew just hoped he wouldn’t be carved into a jack-o’-lantern.
