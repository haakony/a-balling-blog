Croquet ball

TLDR

A croquet ball named Bartholomew gets stuck between a gnome and petunias, jeopardizing the annual tournament. A terrier’s digging inadvertently frees him, but sends him splashing into the birdbath. Bartholomew accepts his fate with a croquet ball’s characteristic dry wit.

The croquet ball, Bartholomew, was having a crisis. Not an existential one, mind you. Bartholomew was a perfectly content croquet ball, enjoying his life of gentle taps and strategic placement on the meticulously manicured lawn of Mrs. Higgins’ prize-winning garden. No, Bartholomew’s crisis was far more immediate: he was stuck. Wedged, to be precise, between a gnome and a particularly aggressive patch of petunias.

It had all started with a rogue gust of wind. Bartholomew, caught mid-roll, had been propelled with surprising force, bypassing the wicket and launching himself directly into the gnome’s beard. Now, he was firmly entrenched, a tiny, brightly coloured prisoner of floral and ceramic confinement.

“Oh, bother,” he muttered, which is what croquet balls do when they’re inconvenienced.

The problem wasn’t just the embarrassment of being stuck. Mrs. Higgins’ annual Croquet Tournament was tomorrow! Bartholomew was a key player, known for his impeccable trajectory and uncanny ability to avoid the mallet. His absence would be catastrophic.

Suddenly, a small, fluffy terrier named Winston, notorious for his love of digging, appeared. Winston, mistaking Bartholomew for a particularly stubborn root, began to furiously excavate around him. The petunias trembled. The gnome looked on, impassive.

With a final, triumphant scrabble, Winston dislodged Bartholomew. He shot out, not towards the wicket, but directly into the birdbath, where he bobbed gently, looking utterly defeated. “Well,” Bartholomew sighed, “at least I’m clean.”
